About

Grounded in data, driven by health equity

Fatima is a researcher with years of experience in research and development. She is interested in maternal and child health, health systems, health equity, global health, and evidence-informed decision-making. She is skilled in quantitative and qualitative studies, as well as data collection and management, and holds a PhD in Epidemiology and a Master of Public Health, both from Tehran University of Medical Sciences.

She currently serves as a Researcher at Bayero University Kano's Africa Centre of Excellence for Population Health & Policy (ACEPHAP), where she coordinates multi-state maternal and child health projects — from sample registration systems that track maternal mortality to quality-of-care studies across northern Nigeria.

Fluent in English and Hausa, she works comfortably across academic, government and community settings.

  • Designs and implements quantitative and qualitative surveys and formative research that inform programming and policy decisions
  • Expert user of STATA; intermediate in SPSS, Nvivo and Atlas.ti
  • Builds data collection tools that improve quality, accountability and efficiency in tracking progress
  • Reviews, refines and monitors key performance indicators across complex, multi-source data
  • Leads and coaches multidisciplinary field and research teams toward shared organizational goals
  • Delivers within scope, budget and schedule, with close attention to detail and accountability
